📐 Best camera angle for accurate detection
  • Frame the whole bed. The print plate should fill most of the view, roughly head-on or at a slight downward angle (~15–30°) — not edge-on.
  • Keep it well lit and in focus. Even, diffuse light; avoid glare, deep shadow, and backlight from a window.
  • Mount it steady and clear of the moving gantry, so frames aren't blurred or blocked mid-print.
  • Show the print, not just the machine. If the nozzle or a part hides the object, the model can't judge it — favor a side that keeps the part visible.
  • Plain background. Fewer competing objects behind the printer means fewer false "spaghetti" reads.
